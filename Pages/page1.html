{Heading: Visuals} {Paragraph 1} {While the original’s visuals are clearly dated, they fit the dreary otherworldly feeling of Silent Hill. The problem with the remake’s visuals is that it’s clearly trying to show off just how advanced modern graphics can be, but forgets that most of the game wasn’t supposed to look pretty in the first place.} {Paragraph 2} {The original had drab and washed out colors and environments that looked decayed and abandoned. Not only did this suit the unnerving tone of the game, it also made it easier to pick out the details and items that were important, as these elements were visually distinct. In the remake though the colors aren’t as washed out, and the environments are much more cluttered. This not only undermines the tone, but it causes the important details and items to get lost in the visual noise, necessitating the clumsy use of icons and markers to make up for the game’s undisciplined art direction.} {Environments Comparison Pictures} {Left and Right buttons to scroll through different pairs} Apartment Hospital Hotel {Paragraph 3} {The monsters also don’t feel quite as viscerally grotesque and horrifying as they were in the original in terms of how they look, move, and sound. The designs in the original found a good balance between the mundane and the nightmarish, making the monsters feel disturbingly real.} {Paragraph 4} {The remake on the other hand focused less on making the monsters feel real and more on making the monsters scary, which largely fell flat. In general the original monster designs looked and felt like actual inhuman monsters, while the new monster designs look more like people in costumes cosplaying as monsters.
